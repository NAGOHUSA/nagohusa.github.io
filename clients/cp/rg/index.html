Artist Statement

Beauty
as
Direction

"Art, like the flower's sensuous detail, is extra — a North Star guiding our making; an ought."

The extra things around me — earrings, architectural trim, kitsch, pottery, and toys — have only risen further to the surface of my perception and greatly influence these intentionally colorful, bold in shape, rich in pattern artifacts that are a bouquet of attempts at doing 'good' via art.

Several years ago, while working as a rural mailman, I came upon a flowering plant among the dust of the road — it had a quality that rose above mere survival and into another realm altogether. It was beautiful. That lack of necessity is the same quality distilled from art conceptually: its uselessness. Art, like the flower, is extra.

— Robert Grissett

About the Artist

Robert
Grissett

An artist from Southwest Georgia living in Americus with his wife and four children. His journey began with flags, then graffiti, screen printing, and finally art school.

He has shown work at local, regional, and national exhibitions from Georgia to New York.
